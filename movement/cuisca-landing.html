Welcome to CuISCA

Building peace through presence, not power.
Security through solidarity, not force.

Who We Are

CuISCA was founded on the shores of Copper Island with a simple but powerful vision: communities can protect and support each other without resorting to violence. We are a network of trained community members dedicated to building resilience, fostering dialogue, and providing alternatives to militarized security approaches.

Our organization brings together people from all walks of life—teachers, craftspeople, healthcare workers, students, and retirees—united by a commitment to nonviolent principles and community sovereignty. What began as a small local initiative has grown into a regional movement, demonstrating that security through solidarity is not just an ideal, but a practical reality.

Community Preparedness

  • Facilitate workshops on community resilience and emergency response
  • Develop early warning systems for potential conflicts or disasters
  • Create community-specific security protocols through participatory processes
  • Provide training in mediation, de-escalation, and conflict transformation

Active Nonviolent Protection

  • Deploy trained teams to areas experiencing or at risk of conflict
  • Offer protective accompaniment to vulnerable individuals or groups
  • Maintain visible peaceful presence in tense situations
  • Document events to increase accountability and transparency

Resource Coordination & Healing

Resource Coordination: Connect communities with essential resources during crises, establish mutual aid networks, support local self-sufficiency initiatives, and facilitate skill-sharing between communities.

Healing and Reconciliation: Implement restorative justice processes, provide trauma-informed support, create spaces for dialogue between groups with historical tensions, and support community-led rebuilding efforts.

Our Approach

At CuISCA, we recognize that lasting security cannot be imposed from outside or achieved through force. Instead, we work alongside communities, respecting their wisdom and autonomy while offering tools, training, and support.

Proactive rather than reactive

We believe in building strong foundations rather than waiting for crises to occur.

Locally rooted but widely connected

Each CuISCA chapter reflects its local context while drawing strength from our broader network.

Practical yet principled

We combine idealism with pragmatism, offering concrete alternatives to violent security paradigms.

Trauma-informed and healing-centered

We recognize that building true security requires addressing past harms and supporting collective healing.

Our Impact

Since our founding, CuISCA has:

  • Trained over 500 community members in nonviolent protection strategies
  • Supported 12 communities through various crises without a single incident of escalated violence
  • Facilitated 30+ successful community dialogue processes addressing deep-rooted conflicts
  • Established sustainable mutual aid networks that continue to function independently
  • Demonstrated viable alternatives to traditional security structures in both rural and urban contexts

But statistics tell only part of the story. Our true impact lies in transformed relationships, strengthened communities, and the gradual shift toward a culture that values cooperation over domination.

"When I established CuISCA, many called it idealistic to imagine communities keeping each other safe without weapons or force. Years later, we've shown it's not only possible but more effective. True security comes not from fear or dominance, but from connection and care. Each day, I witness ordinary people doing extraordinary things simply by choosing another path—one of patience, understanding, and unfailing commitment to each other's dignity. This is the foundation of everything we build."

— Ronan Byrne, Founder

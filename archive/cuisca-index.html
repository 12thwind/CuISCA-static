Where Waters Shape Stone

Like the shifting currents of Lake Superior that continuously shape the shores of the Keweenaw Peninsula, CuISCA embodies the dance between steadfast presence and graceful adaptation. We are a spiritual community dedicated to mindful living, sacred ceremony, and deep connection with the natural world.

Founded on Michigan's "Copper Island," our practices are informed by ancient wisdom traditions and contemporary understanding, the rhythms of the natural world around us, and the unique spiritual journeys of our community members. We offer a sanctuary for those seeking authentic relationship with themselves, each other, and the sacred—however you define it.

Sacred Ceremony

We craft ceremonies that honor life's significant transitions—from weddings and commitment ceremonies to seasonal celebrations, house blessings, and rites of passage. Each ceremony is a unique collaboration, designed to reflect the authentic essence of the individuals involved while connecting with the power of place.

Core Principles

Sacred Relationship with Place

Deep connection with the land and waters that sustain us is essential to spiritual well-being. The Keweenaw Peninsula, with its ancient rock formations, dense forests, and the vast expanse of Lake Superior, serves as both teacher and sanctuary.

Conscious Presence

The cultivation of mindful awareness lies at the heart of our spiritual practice. We believe that presence—the ability to fully inhabit each moment with open awareness—is both the path and the destination.

Interconnected Wholeness

We recognize the fundamental interconnection of all beings and systems. What affects one affects all, and healing occurs through the recognition and restoration of these relationships.

Cyclical Wisdom

Nature reveals that all things move in cycles—seasons, tides, lives. We embrace these natural rhythms rather than fighting against them, finding wisdom in times of both growth and fallowness, joy and grief, gathering and solitude.

Embodied Spirituality

We believe that spiritual wisdom must be lived, not merely contemplated. Our bodies and our daily actions are sacred vessels through which our values are expressed. We seek integration rather than compartmentalization.

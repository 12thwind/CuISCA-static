Community Practices

At CuISCA, we believe that spiritual growth flourishes in community. While individual practice forms an essential foundation, gathering with others creates a container for deeper exploration, mutual support, and collective wisdom. Our community practices offer accessible entry points for people at all stages of their spiritual journey, regardless of background or prior experience.

Each gathering is designed to balance structure with spontaneity, ancient wisdom with contemporary relevance, and individual experience with collective resonance. Our practices honor the rhythms of nature, the wisdom of the body, and the unique gifts that each participant brings to the circle.

All are welcome to participate in these offerings. While some gatherings request a suggested donation to support our work, no one is turned away for lack of funds, and many events are freely offered.

Our Gathering Approach

  • Circle Format - Most gatherings use a circle structure where each person is equally valued
  • Participatory Culture - We encourage active engagement rather than passive consumption
  • Nature Connection - Many gatherings take place outdoors or incorporate natural elements
  • Inclusive Language - We use language that welcomes diverse spiritual perspectives
  • Body Wisdom - We honor the body as a source of knowledge and intuition
  • Both/And Thinking - We embrace paradox and multiple ways of knowing
  • Seasonal Awareness - Our gathering rhythm follows natural cycles and seasonal shifts

Regular Practices

Meditation Circle

Our weekly meditation gatherings offer space for both guided and silent practice in community. Each session includes gentle movement to prepare the body, a variety of meditation techniques drawn from diverse traditions, and opportunity for reflection and integration.

Beginners are warmly welcomed and given clear guidance, while experienced practitioners find depth and new perspectives. The circle format allows for both structure and organic responsiveness to what emerges in the moment.

When: Wednesday evenings, 7:00-8:30pm
Where: Sacred Circle Center (indoor) during winter months; various outdoor locations during warmer seasons
Offering: Suggested donation $5-15, no one turned away

New & Full Moon Gatherings

Following the lunar cycle, these bi-monthly gatherings create space for intention-setting (new moon) and celebration/gratitude (full moon). Each gathering includes ceremony, reflection practices, and community connection, with themes that respond to seasonal energies and collective needs.

New moon gatherings tend toward quieter introspection and visioning, while full moon gatherings embrace more expressive celebration. Both honor the cyclical nature of all things and our relationship with celestial rhythms.

When: New and Full Moons, typically evenings
Where: Varies by season—beaches, forests, or indoor spaces
Offering: Freely given; contributions to community meal welcome

Nature Connection Experiences

These guided immersion experiences help participants deepen their relationship with the natural world through sensory awareness, naturalist knowledge, and contemplative practices. Each experience focuses on a particular landscape (forest, shoreline, wetland) or theme (bird language, plant relationships, seasonal transitions).

Led by skilled facilitators with extensive ecological knowledge and nature connection experience, these offerings balance science with spiritual awareness, creating a holistic approach to environmental relationship.

When: Monthly, typically weekend mornings
Where: Various natural areas throughout the Keweenaw
Offering: Sliding scale $15-30, scholarships available

Sacred Movement

Honoring the body as a vessel of wisdom, these gatherings explore contemplative movement practices from various traditions. Sessions might include gentle yoga, qigong, authentic movement, ecstatic dance, or other somatic practices that foster embodied awareness.

All movement offerings are adaptable to different physical abilities and emphasize internal experience over external form. No prior movement experience is necessary, and each gathering includes guidance for modifications to suit individual needs.

When: Monday mornings, 7:00-8:15am
Where: Sacred Circle Center or beach/forest locations in summer
Offering: Suggested donation $5-15, no one turned away

Community Potluck & Council

These monthly gatherings combine the nourishment of shared food with the practice of council—a structured form of compassionate communication where each person has the opportunity to speak and be heard from the heart. Each council has a theme or focusing question that invites authentic sharing.

The combination of breaking bread together and intentional dialogue creates a foundation for meaningful community connection that extends beyond spiritual practice into everyday relationship.

When: First Friday of each month, 6:00-9:00pm
Where: Sacred Circle Center
Offering: Bring a dish to share

Study & Practice Groups

These time-limited groups (typically 4-8 weeks) explore specific spiritual texts, practices, or themes in greater depth. Past topics have included dreamwork, contemplative writing, grief tending, traditional crafts as spiritual practice, and exploring cross-cultural wisdom traditions.

Groups combine intellectual exploration with experiential practice, personal reflection with group dialogue. Each group is co-created by participants, with facilitation that encourages shared ownership of the learning journey.

When: Varies by group, typically weekday evenings
Where: Sacred Circle Center or members' homes
Offering: Varies by group; materials fee sometimes applies

Seasonal Celebrations

Following our principle of Cyclical Wisdom, we gather eight times throughout the year to mark the solar cycle—solstices, equinoxes, and the midpoints between them. These celebrations help us attune to the changing energies of the seasons, honor the lessons each offers, and strengthen our community bonds through shared ritual.

Winter Solstice
December 21

Honoring the longest night and the return of light. A time for deep rest, reflection on the past year, and kindling hope in darkness.

Imbolc
February 2

Celebrating the first stirrings of spring beneath snow. A time for awakening creativity, setting intentions, and honoring inner wisdom.

Spring Equinox
March 20

Marking the balance of light and dark. A time for new beginnings, planting seeds of intention, and celebrating renewal.

Beltane
May 1

Celebrating the full flowering of spring. A time for play, passion, and honoring the life force in its abundant expression.

Summer Solstice
June 21

Honoring the longest day and fullness of light. A time for celebration, gratitude for abundance, and exploring our fullest expression.

Lammas
August 1

Celebrating first harvest and ripening. A time for gratitude, recognizing what we've cultivated, and sharing our gifts.

Fall Equinox
September 22

Marking the balance of light and dark. A time for harvesting, giving thanks, and preparing for the inward turn.

Samhain
October 31

Honoring ancestors and the thinning of veils. A time for remembrance, releasing what no longer serves, and deepening relationship with mystery.

About Our Seasonal Celebrations

While these dates correspond to traditional Celtic and European seasonal markers, our celebrations integrate multiple cultural perspectives and focus on the direct experience of seasonal shifts in our unique Keweenaw landscape. Each gathering includes ritual elements, community connection, seasonal foods, and opportunities for both celebration and reflection.

All seasonal celebrations are open to the community, with details announced on our calendar and newsletter. Family-friendly adaptations are available for most celebrations.

Participation Guidelines

  • No prior experience necessary for most gatherings
  • Arrive 5-10 minutes early to settle in
  • Dress for the weather for outdoor gatherings
  • Bring your own water bottle and any personal items needed
  • Children welcome at family-designated events
  • Service animals welcome; please notify us in advance
  • Fragrance-free space to support those with sensitivities
